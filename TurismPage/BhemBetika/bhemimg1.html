BhimBetka Rock

Smarthistory – Bhimbetka cave paintingsThe Bhimbetka rock shelters, a UNESCO World Heritage Site located in Madhya Pradesh, India, contain over 750 shelters (500 with paintings) documenting human habitation from the Paleolithic to the Medieval period. Dated over 30,000 years old, the paintings portray daily life, hunting, dancing, and animals using natural pigments like red hematite and manganese.

Location:

Southern edge of the central Indian plateau in the Vindhyan Mountains, Raisen District, near Bhopal.

Discovery:

Discovered in 1956–57 by archaeologist Dr. V.S. Wakankar.

Zoo Rock :

A massive rock shelter displaying a large number of animal figures including elephants, bison, deer, and peacocks.

Auditorium Cave:

Considered the most significant site with massive rock structures and early cup-like depressions dating back ~100,000 years.
